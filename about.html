🇮🇳 The Problem

India is the diabetes capital of the world — and most people don't know they're at risk

136 million Indians have diabetes. Another estimated 136 million are prediabetic — and the vast majority have no idea. Prediabetes causes no symptoms. It doesn't hurt. It doesn't show up in a mirror. But left undetected, it almost always progresses to Type 2 diabetes within 5–10 years.

Why this is a crisis right now

  • South Asians develop diabetes at lower BMI thresholds than Western populations — standard BMI charts miss us
  • The average Indian waits until symptoms appear before getting tested — by then, damage has started
  • A full HbA1c blood test costs ₹300–600 and requires a doctor visit — most people skip it for years
  • Prediabetes is fully reversible with lifestyle changes — but only if caught in time
  • India spends ₹5 lakh crore annually managing diabetes complications — prevention costs a fraction

✅ What We Do

A 3-minute screening that checks what your BMI misses

DiabFit is a clinically validated risk screening tool built on the ADA/WHO FINDRISC questionnaire, adapted specifically for India. It checks 17 risk factors — including family history, blood pressure, sleep, stress, waist size, and previous blood sugar history — that a simple BMI reading completely ignores.

In 3 minutes, anyone in India can know their real prediabetes risk level, get a shareable certificate card, and if at risk, receive a personalised 30-day Indian diet and activity plan to start reversing that risk today.

⚖️ Medical Disclaimer

What DiabFit is — and what it is not

DiabFit is a risk screening tool, not a diagnostic device. It uses the validated ADA/WHO FINDRISC questionnaire to estimate prediabetes risk based on self-reported information. It does not diagnose prediabetes or diabetes. Only a qualified doctor and a blood test (HbA1c or fasting plasma glucose) can provide a medical diagnosis.

If DiabFit shows moderate or high risk, we strongly encourage you to book an HbA1c test and consult your doctor. The 30-day plan we provide is general wellness guidance — not medical advice or a treatment protocol.
